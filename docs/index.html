Key Insights for Advisory Board Members

1. The Barrier Is Organisational, Not Technical

The biggest challenge in AI adoption isn't the technology itself—AI tools are increasingly accessible and powerful. The real challenge is figuring out where humans should be in the loop: Where do we review? Where do we monitor? Where do we set strategic boundaries?

Why this matters to you: This is exactly what your organisations are navigating. The frameworks we're developing in education mirror the organisational design questions you're solving in business.

2. From Hesitation to Confident Practice

Six months ago, faculty were asking "Should I use AI?" Today, they're asking "How do I use it well?" This shift represents a fundamental change in mindset—from fear and uncertainty to strategic experimentation.

Why this matters to you: Graduates entering your workplaces will increasingly have practiced AI use during their education. They won't be starting from zero—they'll have frameworks for critical evaluation and experience with AI-enabled workflows.

3. Assessment 2030: Demonstration vs. Prevention

Traditional approaches to academic integrity (preventing AI use) are giving way to new models where assessment is designed so that AI use reveals rather than hides student thinking. The 50/50 principle: at least 50% of assessment should demonstrate individual student thinking, with AI as a potential tool, not a replacement.

Why this matters to you: This mirrors workplace reality. You need employees who can use AI effectively while remaining accountable for outcomes. Our assessment redesign prepares students for exactly that environment.

4. Industry Partnership Opportunities

Faculty need real-world examples of AI transformation—both successes and failures. Students need practice in contexts that mirror actual workplaces. We can't design curriculum for your industries without understanding your experience.

What we're seeking: Case studies, internships with AI components, guest perspectives on AI adoption challenges, and guidance on what AI capabilities you actually need in new hires.

About Assessment 2030

Assessment 2030 is a Curtin-wide initiative rethinking assessment design in the AI era. Rather than trying to prevent AI use through detection and prohibition, the project explores how to design authentic assessment where AI can be a tool without becoming a shortcut.

Core Principles:

  • Demonstration over Prevention: Design assessment where students demonstrate their thinking, process, and understanding
  • The 50/50 Principle: At least 50% of any assessment should require demonstrable individual student thinking
  • Authentic Context: Assessment that mirrors real-world AI-enabled workflows
  • Process Evaluation: Assess the journey, not just the final product
